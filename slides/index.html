ZKMAO

What is Mao?

Mao is a card game in which players compete to get rid of all their hand. It has the following rules:

  • A player can only put a card down if it is the same suit or rank to the previous one
  • Players are penalized if they do not follow rules that depend on the history of the game.
  • The winner of each round gets to add a new secret rule that the other players need to abide by (by trial and error).

Why ZK?

Zero knoweledge allows players to trust that the person penalizing them is actually following a predecided rule.

Our Project

We developed a two-player simplified version of Mao, the game goes as follows:

  • At the end of each round, players randomly draw tokens from a set of personal tokens, the drawn token determines the penalty of the player's next rule. (the random scheme is also based on snarks)
  • Rules are restricted to being binary, and to only consider the last two moves

A Live demo

How does it work?

  • A new rule is encoded as a bitmap (using the bits of 29 integers).
  • A hash of the rule is shared with the other player.
  • In each step, each player verifies whether the other broke one of his rules, and produces a proof that a rule with the hash he shared is broken.

    • Limits and future development?
